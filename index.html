Brand Usage — Do’s & Don’ts

Do

  • Use the exact logo files from this guide — the two arcs + bolt are the mark.
  • Keep stroke width 5 and round linecaps in every reproduction.
  • Use the white monochrome variant on dark backgrounds.
  • Use Brand Cyan #0EA5E9 for CTAs, links and accents; Navy #0B2347 for headings.
  • Set headings in Outfit, body copy in Inter, numerics/code in JetBrains Mono.
  • Give the mark clear space of at least the bolt’s width on all sides.

Don’t

  • Never redraw, re-trace or approximate the mark.
  • Never omit the bolt or use a plain-bolt logo — the ring + bolt combination is the mark.
  • Never change stroke width or caps (no width 6, no butt caps).
  • Never recolor the mark outside the approved variants (primary, white, navy, cyan).
  • Never recolor the ChargeControl mark with partner colors.
  • Never use legacy blue #2563eb on branded surfaces.
  • Never stretch, rotate, outline, or add effects (shadows, gradients) to the mark.

Tone of Voice

How ChargeControl sounds. The market is Dutch-first — we write Dutch copy for Dutch audiences and keep established English product terms (smart charging, ChargeBack, Home Energy Hub) untranslated. One brand voice, tuned per audience.

Voice principles

  • Clear over clever. Energy is complicated; our language isn't. Short sentences, active voice, one idea per sentence.
  • Confident, never hyped. We state what the platform does. No "revolutionary", no exclamation-mark enthusiasm.
  • Helpful, human, direct. We talk to people ("you/je"), not at "users". We say what happens next.
  • Precise with numbers. Money, energy and savings always carry units and honest framing — see Data Visualization for formatting.
  • Dutch-first. Consumer copy defaults to Dutch (informal "je"); business and investor copy may use "u" or English. Established English product terms stay in English in every language.

Per audience

AudienceRegisterFocus onAvoid
Drivers & consumersFriendly, informal (NL "je")What it saves and does for them, in plain language; protocols stay invisibleAcronyms (OCPP, V2G, DLB) without explanation; technical detail they didn't ask for
Fleet managers & partnersProfessional, concreteControl, cost, compliance, reporting; correct technical terms are welcomeConsumer fluff; vague promises without numbers
InvestorsFactual, preciseVerified facts and clearly-labelled projectionsHype adjectives; unverified claims; mixing actuals with forecasts

Examples

Write like this

  • "Je auto laadt automatisch op de goedkoopste uren. Gemiddeld bespaar je zo tot 30% op je laadkosten." (consumer — plain, concrete)
  • "ChargeBack reimburses home charging automatically — verified sessions, one monthly payout, full audit trail." (business — control & proof)
  • "Charging pauses when your home's power draw peaks, and resumes automatically." (explains DLB without the acronym)

Not like this

  • "Our revolutionary AI-driven OCPP 2.0.1 smart charging solution disrupts the e-mobility landscape!" (hype + acronym soup)
  • "The user should configure their DLB parameters in the portal." ("the user", unexplained acronym)
  • "Bespaar gigantisch veel geld!!!" (vague claim, hype punctuation)

Naming is part of voice: always follow the Naming Rules — "Home Energy Hub" (never abbreviations), "ChargeBack" with capital C and B, "Sparky AI advisor".

Accessibility & Contrast

Minimum rules so branded surfaces stay legible for everyone. Target is WCAG 2.1 AA: contrast ≥ 4.5:1 for normal text, ≥ 3:1 for large text (≥ 24px, or ≥ 19px bold) and UI graphics.

Rules

  • Cyan #0EA5E9 is not a text color on white. Use it for icons, charts, fills and large graphic elements; for link or label text on white, use deep cyan #0369A1.
  • Green and amber never carry text on light backgrounds — use them as status fills/badges with navy or white text that passes AA.
  • Minimum text sizes: body 16px, secondary/captions 14px, never below 12px anywhere. Line-height ≥ 1.5 for body copy.
  • Focus states: every interactive element shows a visible focus ring — 2px cyan #0EA5E9 outline with 2px offset (the ring is a graphic, so 3:1 against white is satisfied at ≥ 2px thickness with the offset gap).
  • Never encode meaning in color alone — pair status colors with an icon or label (e.g. ✓ / ⚠ text).
  • Text over photos needs a navy scrim (≥ 60% opacity) or a solid panel — never white text straight on a busy image.

Paper-friendly variants

  • 1-color print: use the monochrome navy icon/lockup (PMS 282 C or 100% K as fallback). The cyan monochrome variant is for accent use on white coated stock only.
  • On dark or navy stock: use the white monochrome variant — never the primary two-color mark on navy.
  • Uncoated / newsprint: prefer the navy monochrome mark; large solid cyan areas shift towards teal on uncoated paper — avoid full-bleed cyan there.
  • Documents: body text prints in navy or black, headings in Outfit, body in Inter — same hierarchy as digital.

Chart rules

  • Titles in Outfit 600, labels in Inter, all numerals in JetBrains Mono.
  • Bar and area charts always start the value axis at zero; gridlines #E2E8F0, no chart borders or 3D effects.
  • Positive/negative or status coloring: green #22C55E for good/savings, amber #F59E0B for warnings, never red/green as the only differentiator (add labels or icons).
  • On dark surfaces, swap navy series to light cyan #7DD3FC and gridlines to #1E293B.
  • Legends only when there is more than one series; prefer direct labeling.
